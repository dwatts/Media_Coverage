Resurrection City

Digital Atlas

Resurrection City was the product of a truly national movement of activists building on the African American civil rights protests of the 1950s-60s. This represented a multiracial movement, championed by recently assassinated Dr. Martin Luther King Jr. and realized by the thousands of residents in Resurrection City. Newspapers, radio programs, television stations, photographers, and independent journalists from across the country traveled to cover Resurrection City and the Poor People's Campaign.

While Resurrection City was emerging on the Mall, what was the rest of the US hearing and what did they think about it? What were folks reading and seeing in your hometown?

National Media Coverage

This map samples some of the media coverage shared with folks in every corner of the US, including Resurrection City's own newsletter and radio program. Some of these newspaper articles demonstrate the antipathy towards the movement. Many discuss the complexity of issues like nonviolence, systemic poverty, and permitting on the National Mall.
